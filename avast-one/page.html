Resume

Rebuilding trust through a new 360 protection

About 

At that time, Avast was facing trust issues after data sharing controversies.

Challenge 

Regain user trust by making privacy core, replacing dark patterns and rebranding grumpy product into a friendly one.

Impact

Proved that even compex tech can fee simple and human, (saIes) while rebuilding trust and introducing new modular design system.

TL;DR

Clear, non-intrusive upsell

Tech-heavy product that feels playfully human

Positive industry recognition (e.g. PCMag, TechRadar, Serious Insights)

Design process

  1. Collecting and synthesising data

I joined the project after initial research phase. I connected findings from this research with my product knowledge and legacy learning to uncover patterns in user needs, trust gaps, and business opportunities.

These insights shaped the first ideas of what the product should deliver, how it should be structured, and which ideas to explore further in brainstorming.

  1. Wireframing structure and flows

We defined how the individual protection tools should connect into one product by mapping the core structure, navigation and the key flows. My focus was on balancing the simplicity for everyday users with the depth needed by advanced users as well as with the business goals.

We defined how the individual protection tools should connect into one product by mapping the core structure, navigation and the key flows. My focus was on balancing the simplicity for everyday users with the depth needed by advanced users as well as with the business goals.

  1. Testing and iterations

We turned early sketches into UI prototypes using our initial design system.

At this stage, we split ownership across product features while staying aligned as a team. I focused on privacy (VPN and password monitoring).

We ran usability sessions to validate our work and brainstormed improvements. Key change was a progressive structure. Everyday users could access core settings at the surface level, while power users could dive deeper to change advanced configurations.

Testing also revealed that our design system needs lighter, more human tone to better communicate trust and simplicity we aimed for.

  1. Collaboration the the sales team

Later, we released a Beta version to validate the experience in real-world use. In parallel, I collaborated with the sales team to replace dark pattern tactics, and introduce a transparent, trustworthy experience that still supports business goals. This was not to undermining trust to help Avast rebuild its reputation.

For example, I designed VPN reminders to appear only when relevant, with clear, not fear based language and an opt-out to balance user control with business opportunity.

Reflection and learning

This project taught me that building trust goes far beyond UI.

In a sales-driven environment, it required constant alignment with sales pushing back on aggressive tactics, advocating for users, and ensuring every message was genuinely helpful. I also learned how important it is to plug UX effort to existing business metrics, helping the sales team see the value of design in their own language.

It was my first experience designing a complex product from the ground up, where I learned to prioritize information, define navigation logic, and structure simplicity within depth.

Finally, I realized how crucial it is to create alignment early rather than wait for it early alignment saves time, rework, and confusion later.
