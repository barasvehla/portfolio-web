Bringing clarity to chaotic Calendars

About 

Wrike launched a new Calendar while a similar feature already existed.

Challenge 

Two similar features confused users and raised costs. Can one replace the other without losing users?

Impact

One calendar replacing legacy tool while all key use cases kept. Sustainable delivery. Higher adoption.

TL;DR

🔄 Simplified core flows

🛠 Phased rollout & realistic planning

📈 Higher adoption & stickiness

Design process

  1. Challenge and goals

As first, I made sure I fully understand the problem, its context, all the whys and expected outcomes.

Then I set up goals and scope:

  • Define calendars’ direction and the steps to get there

  • Simplify core flows we knew from data are problematic to increase retention

  • Improve onboarding to reduce drop-off

  1. Research and synthesis

Sneak peek into my research: I started with internal data, then added interviews and usability tests, as it was clear deeper insights were needed.

  1. Rollout and execution

I tested an MVP with users, validated, iterated and worked with the PM and devs on phased rollout.

Reflection and learning

Two years later, the old tool still exists and some critical improvements were never implemented, as priorities shifted. This reminded me that UX is not only about beautiful screens and it never really ends. It’s about pushing for critical needs, proving impact, and staying pragmatic on the long journey.
